3. Full Description (~300 words)
Horsera is the first AI-powered rider development platform for equestrian athletes built to turn every ride into structured, measurable progress.
More than 7 million riders in the US invest enormous time, effort, and resources into training, yet riders still lack a clear system for understanding progress and guide development over time. Training insights are often scattered across lesson feedback, ride notes, training videos, and competition results, making it difficult for riders to clearly understand where they are in their development journey, see patterns in their development or know what to focus on next.
Horsera brings these pieces together into one intelligent system designed around the rider’s development journey. The platform connects goals, rides, video analysis, feedback, learning resources, and structured progression frameworks into a single environment that helps riders understand where they are in their development and which skills to focus on next to progress.
Because riding is a lifelong discipline where athletes continually refine their skills over many years, understanding development over time becomes especially important. By mapping training data and feedback to these progression pathways, Horsera uses AI to make development visible over time and generate objective, evidence-based insight into rider progress. Riders can track their development historically, identify limiting factors in their riding, and gain a clearer sense of when they are ready to advance to the next level of training or competition. Horsera also includes an AI companion that helps interpret training data, surface patterns, and provide ongoing recommendations, giving riders an always-available source of guidance between lessons.
At the core of Horsera is a continuous development loop where each ride generates insight for the next. Instead of disconnected training sessions, riding becomes a structured cycle of learning and improvement that compounds over time.
Over time, Horsera has the potential to become the longitudinal system of record for rider–horse development, forming the foundation for an intelligent operating system that connects riders, trainers, training data, and performance insights across the equestrian ecosystem.
As a rider myself, I experienced this challenge firsthand and saw the opportunity to bring modern technology and AI-driven insight to a sport that has remained largely underserved by digital training tools.

4. The Problem
More than 7 million riders in the United States invest enormous time, dedication, and financial resources into training and competition, yet riders still lack a clear system for understanding progress and guide development over time. Riding is a lifelong discipline where athletes continually refine their skills over many years, making long-term visibility into progress especially valuable. Training insights are often scattered across lesson feedback, ride notes, training videos, and competition results, making it difficult to see patterns in development or identify the skills that matter most for progression.
As a result, riders often struggle to understand where they are in their development journey, what is holding them back, or what they should focus on next to progress. Riders frequently ask themselves questions like: What skill should I focus on next? Am I ready to move up? Why does progress sometimes feel unclear? Am I actually improving over time?
Without a clear development structure, riders lack direction. Training becomes a series of disconnected rides, feedback is fragmented and difficult to act on, progress is hard to see over time, and readiness to advance remains unclear.

5. The Solution
Horsera transforms the rider development journey into a connected, intelligent system.
The platform brings together goals, rides, video analysis, feedback, learning resources, and structured progression pathways into a single environment designed around how riders actually develop over time.
Instead of treating rides, lessons, and competitions as isolated events, Horsera organizes training into a structured progression that helps riders understand where they are in their development and what skills to focus on next to continue improving. Riders can follow established progression pathways or define their own goals, allowing training to align with personal ambitions, skill development, and competitive milestones.
By capturing ride data, feedback, and video insights in one place, Horsera uses AI to make development visible over time and generate objective, evidence-based insight into rider progress. At the heart of Horsera is Cadence — a persistent AI advisor that builds a deep, longitudinal understanding of each rider. Cadence knows your goals, your history, your patterns, and your horse. The more you ride, the smarter it gets.
 Riders can track their progress historically, identify patterns in their riding, and better understand which factors are helping them improve or holding them back.
The platform also introduces clearer signals for readiness. By analyzing training history and performance patterns, AI-powered insights help riders understand when they are prepared to advance to the next stage of training, attempt a new level, or enter competition with confidence.
At the core of Horsera is a continuous development loop. Each ride generates insight that informs the next—connecting goals, practice, feedback, and learning into a structured cycle of improvement. Over time, this loop transforms everyday riding from a series of isolated sessions into a deliberate habit of compounding progress.
The Horsera Development Loop: Turning Every Ride Into Progress
Horsera transforms riding into a continuous development loop where each ride generates insight that informs the next.
🎯  Set Goals    →    🏇 Ride & Practice    →    📹 Capture Evidence    →    🤖 AI Analysis    →    💡 Insight & Guidance    →    📈 Progress & Readiness    →     (back to Ride)
1️⃣ Set Goals: Riders define development goals, training priorities, or progression milestones.
2️⃣ Ride & Practice: Training sessions, lessons, and competitions generate real-world riding experience.
3️⃣ Capture Evidence: Ride logs, trainer feedback, and video recordings capture what happened during the ride.
4️⃣ AI Analysis: Horsera analyzes ride data, video insights, and feedback to surface patterns and signals, including evaluation of discipline-specific test performances
5️⃣ Insight & Guidance: The system identifies progress, highlights limiting factors, and suggests what to focus on next.
6️⃣ Progress & Readiness: Riders see their progress over time and gain clearer signals about readiness to advance.
Then the loop repeats: → Next Ride informed by the previous one

6. Why Now
Advances in artificial intelligence, video analysis, and mobile technology are making it possible to analyze athletic performance in ways that were previously accessible only to elite athletes with specialized coaching resources.
These technologies are now transforming how athletes train across many sports, enabling more structured, data-driven approaches to skill development and performance improvement.
Yet despite its global scale and economic significance, equestrian sport has remained largely underserved by modern training technology. Millions of riders train regularly, record lessons and competitions on their phones, and seek ways to improve, but few tools exist to help them meaningfully interpret that information or understand their long-term development.
Horsera brings these capabilities to everyday riders by combining structured training progression, video analysis, feedback, and AI-powered insights into one platform designed specifically for equestrian development.
While many tools exist for logging rides or watching training videos, Horsera focuses on something deeper: helping riders truly understand their development, track progress over time, and accelerate improvement through a structured system of learning and feedback.
Together, these shifts create a unique opportunity to bring modern performance intelligence to a sport that has historically relied on fragmented tools and informal development pathways.

7. Long-Term Vision
Horsera begins with a focused but powerful goal: becoming the longitudinal system of record for rider–horse development.
By capturing the full history of training, feedback, performance, and progression over time, Horsera creates a trusted foundation for understanding how riders and horses truly develop. Because riding is a lifelong discipline where athletes continually refine their skills, this long-term development record becomes increasingly valuable over time.
From this foundation, Horsera can unlock a new layer of performance intelligence for the equestrian world. By analyzing patterns across training, biomechanics, feedback, readiness signals, and competitive outcomes, the platform can help riders, trainers, and programs better understand how development actually unfolds and how to accelerate progress.
As the platform grows, Horsera can also become the connective layer across the equestrian ecosystem—bringing riders, trainers, barns, programs, federations, and other stakeholders into a shared system built around development, performance, and horse welfare.
Over time, this foundation opens the door to a wide range of possibilities, including:
• AI-powered coaching and training guidance, providing personalized insights during rides.
• Integration with sensors on horses and riders, enabling deeper analysis of biomechanics, movement quality, workload, and performance signals.
• Digital certifications and progression frameworks, supporting organizations, federations, and educational programs.
• Trusted histories for horses and riders, enabling more transparent buying, selling, and leasing of horses based on verified development records.
• Horse care and welfare insights, helping surface signals related to workload, recovery, and long-term wellbeing.
• Operational tools for barns and training programs, supporting scheduling, billing, workload management, and program visibility.
These opportunities all stem from the same core asset: a trusted, longitudinal record of rider–horse data.
If Horsera succeeds in becoming that foundation, it has the potential to evolve into the intelligent operating system for the equestrian world—one place where development, performance, learning, horse care, operations, and collaboration across the sport come together.

10. Founder Story
I discovered horseback riding a few years ago and completely fell in love with it.
Riding is a beautiful sport, but it is also incredibly complex. Progress takes time, discipline, commitment, and a deep partnership between horse and rider. It is a lifelong discipline where skills are continually refined and small improvements often take years to fully develop.
As I became more immersed in the sport, I noticed something surprising: even though riders invest enormous time, effort, and money into training, there is very little structure to help them truly understand their development over time.
Riders often rely on occasional lessons, scattered ride notes, or videos across different apps. It can be difficult to see patterns, understand what is improving, or know what skill to focus on next.
As someone who works in AI and technology, I began asking a simple question:
Why doesn’t a system exist that helps riders truly understand their progress?
That question ultimately became the foundation for Horsera.
