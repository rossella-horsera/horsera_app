🐎 Position & Stability — Scoring Logic Overview


Component
	What We Measure
	High-Level Logic
	Ideal
	Head Alignment
	Head balance over torso
	Measure head tilt relative to vertical axis and its movement variability over time
	Head centered over shoulders with minimal tilt (±2° range)
	Upper Body Stability
	Torso lean & steadiness
	Calculate torso angle relative to vertical and how much it fluctuates frame-to-frame
	Neutral upright posture with low angle variability
	Hip Stability
	Seat symmetry & pelvic balance
	Measure left/right hip alignment and vertical movement consistency relative to torso
	Even weight distribution, minimal left/right bias (±2–3°)
	Lower Leg Stability
	Lower leg position consistency
	Track ankle position relative to hip/knee and measure drift or swinging
	Quiet leg with minimal forward/back displacement
	Hand Stability
	Rein steadiness & symmetry
	Measure wrist position variability and left/right asymmetry
	Even, steady hands with minimal bounce and balanced height
	Overall Score
Metric
	Logic
	Ideal
	Overall Position & Stability
	Weighted average of the 5 component scores
	100 = stable, balanced, minimal deviation
